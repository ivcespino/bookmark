黒瀬裕子,
KUROSE YUKO

Yuko was both Akira’s muse and his greatest abyss. Known for her beauty, she seemed like a flower in full bloom, a rare and delicate presence that people in her town couldn't help but covet. Yet Yuko’s life was far from a fairy tale. While Spring’s Coffin portrays her as kind, soft, and innocent, reality wore down these traits, and the town slowly chipped away at her purer side. Over time, Yuko became embittered, bearing sadness in her gaze and bitterness in her heart, her hands stained from the hardships of a life spent surviving in a tainted world. She nurtured the very darkness that she grew up in, her soul deeply intertwined with the grim fate of her surroundings.

Once, she believed she’d found someone to lift her from the shadows—a light that would free her from her town’s curse. But the light fled, too afraid of the abyss she carried within. Yet fate brought her back to Akira in the end, her first glimpse of hope. Within their make-believe last moment, she asks Akira, "You're gonna wait until I'm done?"

野副章,
NOZOE AKIRA

A man who would eventually become a novelist under the pen name Esemori Kōsaku. Akira wrote Spring’s Coffin, a novel inspired by the fragments of his past. The book tells the tale of a boy and girl from his hometown who fall in love, yet face constant opposition from everyone around them. The girl, adored by many, becomes the focal point of the town’s jealousy, with everyone trying to pull the pair apart to have a chance with her. This plotline mirrors Akira's reality at thirteen. However, Spring’s Coffin diverges from real life, culminating in a bittersweet ending: the couple commits double suicide, sinking hand in hand into a dark part of the local river named the Lover’s Abyss. Through Akira’s writing, the line between fiction and reality blurs, his story an entanglement of 'what could have been' and 'what really happened,' woven with threads of his own unfulfilled wishes and lingering memories.

The girl, was of course Yuko, and while in reality, they never quite bonded their souls in the Lover's Abyss nor do I think they require it anymore. As in his fantastical rendition of his last moment, he reassures Yuko, "I'll wait for you."
